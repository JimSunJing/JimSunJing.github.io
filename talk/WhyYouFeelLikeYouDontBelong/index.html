Why You Feel Like You Don’t Belong

翻译练习，it’s from a youtube video by Lana Blakely

Surrounding ourselves with different kinds
of people helps us figure out who we are and who we’re not;

身边围绕着各种各样的人们有助于我们认清自己。

where we fit and where we do not fit in

what we value in other people and what we do not value.

到底哪里适合或不适合我们，以及我们如何评价其他人们。

Those things are important to understand about
ourselves, because as human beings we need

to feel a sense of belonging, to feel like
we can relate to and understand other people,

这些事情对于理解我们自己非常重要，因为身为人类我们需要归属感，去感受和理解别人的想法，

and that they can understand us back—it’s
deeply rooted in us.

以及让其他人理解我们。这是深深植入在我们脑中的需求。

But what if you end up feeling like you don’t
belong anywhere?

但如果你觉得自己不属于任何地方该怎么办？

What if you’re at a place that you don’t
want to be, but you don’t know where else to go?

如果你在一个你不想要呆的地方，而你又不知道自己应该去哪里，该怎么办？

In 2018. it was time for me to move back to
Sweden after having lived in the US for two years.

2018年，我从美国留学两年回到了瑞典。

Two years that somehow felt like a lifetime
of experiences and lessons.

那是一个令我学到非常多的两年，感觉像是度过了一生的时间。

But when I landed in Stockholm and got off
the plane, I didn’t feel like home at all.

但当我降落在Stockholm，踏出飞机的我没有感受到家的感觉。

I met with old friends. I went to all the
usual spots—but it felt different

我去见老朋友，去老地方——他(它)们都变了。

There was a feeling of disconnect.

And I remember thinking

What if I don’t belong here anymore?

那是一种被分离的感觉，我当时在想：我是不是已经不属于这里了？

And if I don’t, then where do I belong?

如果我真的不属于这里，我又属于哪里？

Although we’re all in different boats, carrying
different luggage, heading in different directions,

虽然我们在不同的船上，带着不同的行李，前往不同的方向。【比喻】

we do have something in common: we all want
to feel like we belong.

但我们有一点是相同的：我们都渴望归属感。

We need to feel like we belong.

Somewhere. Anywhere.

我们需要感到我们属于哪里。

And yet, the feeling of not belonging is one
of the most universal of all human experiences.

然而，缺乏归属感又是人类最普遍和共同的一种情绪。

And often times, we become so desperate to
belong that we try to mold ourselves into

what we think society or friends or family
expects of us. We force ourselves to become someone we’re not.

为了满足家人和朋友的期望，我们经常绝望地把自己改造成(mold … into)另一个人。

We do things we don’t want to do. We go places we don’t want to go.
We hang out with people we don’t want to hang out with.

我们去一些本不想去的地方，做一些自己讨厌的事，交一些我们不认同的朋友。

We lose ourselves, all in the hopes of
finding our place in this world.

我们在寻求归属的过程中已经失去了自己。

I spent the first few days back in Stockholm
trying to figure out what was wrong.

What was it that had changed? And so I started thinking

刚到Stockholm的几天，我想要找到为什么这里没有家的感觉。是我变了吗？我开始思考：

Perhaps it was my style, and I just needed
to donate some old clothes and get a few new items.

也许是我的风格变了，我需要捐一些衣服再买一些新东西。

So, I did that.

于是我去“断舍离”。

Perhaps it was my room that no longer represented
who I was.

也许是我的房间应该变化了。

So, I ended up turning my room into a project.

所以我开启了房间改造的计划。

I got rid of some items that I felt were only
taking up space and I got some artwork and

plants instead, which was nice and brought
me a temporary feeling of joy, but it wasn’t enough.

我又开始“断舍离”了。这给我带来一些欢乐，但这还不够。

Perhaps I just didn’t belong in Sweden anymore.

But for that one, unlike clothes and decorations,
I couldn’t really find out, at least not yet.

有没有可能我不再适合瑞典了？但这件事不像衣服和房间装饰那样简单，我暂时还没有答案。

I came across a quote by Brené Brown that goes

“Because true belonging only happens when we present our authentic, imperfect selves to the world, our sense of belonging can never be greater than our level of self-acceptance.”

当时我读到Berné Brown的一句话：

“当你把真诚且不完美的自己展现给世界的时候，你才会找到真正的归属感，故我们所能感受到的归属永远不会超过我们的自我接纳水平。”【这句好难翻译】

I started thinking about two things:

First, can you really present your authentic
self unless you know yourself?

我开始考虑两件事：

首先，当一个人都不了解自己，ta 能展现真诚、不完美的自己给世界吗？

Perhaps that was the reason so many people felt
like no one understood them—because they

didn’t really understand themselves.

也许这就是许多人觉得没人能理解自己的原因——他们自己都不了解自己。

And second, how often are we doing that is
expected of us, versus doing what we actually

want to be doing?

其次，我们平时在做多少别人期望自己做的事？我们自己想做的呢？

Here’s why that matters.

这是关键。

We are heavily influenced by our environment.

Everything from the way we think to the way
we behave and feel.

我们受到环境的强烈影响，从我们的思维到我们的行为和感受。

If you’re choosing your environment based
on what you want, you are likely to end up at a place

where you are going to feel a sense of belonging. Either with the people or just with the surroundings.

如果你选择了你自己想要的环境，你有可能最终会找到有归属感的地方或团体。

On the other hand, if you choose your environment
based on what you think is expected of you,

you are likely to end up at a place where you don’t feel like you belong. Not with the people and not with the surroundings.

相反的，如果你选择的环境是其他人期望你选择的，你很可能会陷入你不喜欢的环境。

Think of your job, your education, your group
of friends, your family.

Think of how you spend your days and your
weekends. And ask yourself, how much time are you spending doing what you actually want to be doing? And how much time are you spending doing what is expected of you?

想一想自己的工作、教育、团体、朋友和家人。想想你怎么度过一周，问一下自己你有多少时间在做自己想做的事情，又有多少时间花费在其他人期望你去做的事情？

I decided to focus on those two things.
Getting to know myself better and embracing my own path, not someone else’s.

我觉得关注在这两点上，以尝试更加清晰的认识自己，并去拥抱自己的想法，而不是其他人想要的。

So I started with asking myself questions
about my fears, and insecurities, loves, hopes and dreams.

于是我开始问我自己：我的恐惧、不安、爱恋、希望以及梦想是什么。

If some questions were left unanswered, I
sought them out in books and through experiences,

through trying new things and exploring new
topics.

如果一些问题我无法回答，我会从书中和经验以及通过探索新事物和新的主题寻找答案。

And as for embracing my own path, I stopped
trying to mold myself into someone I was not.

我开始不再把自己塑造成其他人想要的样子。

I wasn’t going to throw a big dinner party
for my birthday because that was expected,

because that’s what you’re supposed to
do on your birthday.

我不再因为他人的期望而为自己的生日举办一个大的party。

I wanted to cook and play board games.

So that’s what I was going to do.

我开始学习烹饪和玩桌游，因为这是我想要的。

As time passed, I eventually started to feel
closer to home.

随着时间的流逝，我开始有了家的感觉。
